Pothole Research App

A research proposal by a University of New Orleans Professor, Dr Phani, was to see how often people were to be tricked by a fake login and give up their private data. To do this we created a pothole tracker app, that seemingly had a purpose to mark different potholes around the city. The catch was that to log in you could either make an account, or use Fakebook, Twitter, or Google. Instead of actually connecting to these individual login services, we made fake login pages for each company and when they log in we store the data to an SQL sheet. We used React to create the app, css to style it, and SQL for the database. For our map we used the GoogleMap API. For this project I implemented the front-end framework to fake login screens. Creating them with React, Styling with CSS, and connecting to databases with JS.
